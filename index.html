Why We Exist?

  • Mental health in India - Over 90 million Indians, or 7.5 percent of the country’s population of 1.3 billion, suffer from some sort of mental disorder, according to the world health organization, less than 4000 experts available, as result, 83 percent of people with mental health disorders do not get treatment.
  • In our country, the discovery of a mental illness is often followed by denial and hesitation to seek help. Even though mental disorder can be controlled, most people tend to sweep their issue under the carpet and suffer in silence.
  • Although India is world’s fifth largest economy, it has spent only 0.05 percent of its health budget annually on mental health over the few years, much lower than even the average spending of low-income countries, which comes to about 0.5 percent of their healthcare budgets.
  • Lack of Awareness: People are not aware that they may be suffering from a mental health concern and so don't take any treatment. Educating people about mental well being however is a battle in itself.
